How to Audit Your Browser Location Permissions

Modern browsers are designed with a "Sandbox" security model. This means a website cannot access your GPS, Camera, or Microphone unless you explicitly grant permission. However, it is easy to click "Block" by accident, or to forget which sites you have allowed.

This guide provides a step-by-step audit to reset or revoke these permissions on the most common platforms.

1. Google Chrome (Desktop & Android)

Chrome allows for per-site settings, which is the safest way to manage access.

  1. Look at the address bar (where the URL is).
  2. Click the Lock Icon ðŸ”’ or the Settings Icon on the far left.
  3. Select "Permissions" or "Site Settings".
  4. Find "Location". You will see options for "Ask (Default)", "Allow", or "Block".
  5. To Reset: Click "Reset permissions" to force the site to ask you again next time.

2. Safari (iOS & iPhone)

Apple handles permissions at the Operating System level, not just the browser level. This adds an extra layer of security.

  1. Open your iPhone Settings app (grey gear icon).
  2. Scroll down to Privacy & Security.
  3. Tap Location Services.
  4. Scroll down to find Safari Websites.
  5. You can choose "While Using the App" (Recommended) or "Never".

3. Mozilla Firefox

Firefox is known for strict privacy defaults. If a site isn't working, Firefox may have blocked the request silently.

  1. Click the Shield Icon or Lock Icon in the address bar.
  2. Look for "Blocked Temporarily" or "Allowed".
  3. Click the X next to "Allowed" to revoke permission immediately.
Security Best Practice: Never enable "Always Allow" for a website unless it is a navigation tool you use daily. We recommend the "Ask Next Time" setting, which ensures you are always conscious of when your location is being accessed.

Why "Ask" is Better Than "Allow"

For maximum security, we recommend keeping your global setting on "Ask Next Time." This ensures that no website can access your GPS coordinates in the background.
