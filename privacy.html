Privacy Policy

Effective Date: February 12, 2026

At TellMyLocation.com, accessible from https://tellmylocation.com, one of our main priorities is the privacy of our visitors. This Privacy Policy document contains types of information that is collected and recorded by TellMyLocation and how we use it.

Core Promise: We operate on a "Client-Side Architecture." This means your GPS coordinates are processed locally on your device and are never sent to a database we control.

1. Information We Collect

Because we do not have user accounts or login systems, the information we collect is minimal and strictly technical.

A. Geolocation Data

When you allow your browser to access your location, we receive your Latitude, Longitude, Altitude, and Heading. This data is stored in your device's Random Access Memory (RAM) for the duration of your session. Once you close the tab, this data is deleted. We do not store a history of your locations.

B. Log Files

Like practically all websites, we use standard log files. These files log visitors when they visit the website. The information collected includes internet protocol (IP) addresses, browser type, Internet Service Provider (ISP), date and time stamp, and referring/exit pages.

2. Third-Party Processors

We use trusted third-party services to provide specific features. These services may process your IP address or coordinates to return data (like a map image or weather report).

  • OpenStreetMap: Used to display the map tiles. They receive coordinate requests to render the correct map area.
  • Open-Meteo API: Used to fetch local weather data. We send your latitude and longitude to their API to retrieve the forecast.
  • Google AdSense: Used to display advertisements.

3. Google AdSense & Cookies

TellMyLocation uses Google AdSense to serve ads. Google uses cookies, including the DoubleClick DART cookie, to serve ads to users based on their visit to our site and other sites on the Internet.

  • Third-party vendors, including Google, use cookies to serve ads based on a user's prior visits to your website or other websites.
  • Users may opt-out of the use of the DART cookie by visiting the Google ad and content network privacy policy at the following URL – https://policies.google.com/technologies/ads

4. GDPR Data Protection Rights (EU)

We would like to make sure you are fully aware of all of your data protection rights. Every user is entitled to the following:

  • The right to access: You have the right to request copies of your personal data. (Note: Since we store no data, we hold no records of you).
  • The right to erasure: You have the right to request that we erase your personal data. (Note: Your data is automatically erased when you close the browser).
  • The right to restrict processing: You can restrict processing by denying Location Permissions in your browser settings.

5. CCPA Privacy Rights (California)

Under the CCPA, among other rights, California consumers have the right to:

  • Request that a business that collects a consumer's personal data disclose the categories and specific pieces of personal data that a business has collected about consumers.
  • Request that a business delete any personal data about the consumer that a business has collected.
  • Request that a business that sells a consumer's personal data, not sell the consumer's personal data. (We do not sell user data).

6. Consent

By using our website, you hereby consent to our Privacy Policy and agree to its Terms and Conditions.
