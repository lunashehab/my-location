Privacy Policy & Data Security

Last Updated: January 2026

This document outlines the technical and legal protocols governing your data on TellMyLocation.com. Unlike standard policies, we explain the lifecycle of your data to demonstrate our commitment to Data Minimization.

1. The Lifecycle of a Coordinate

When you use our service, your location data follows a strict, enclosed path designed to prevent persistence:

1. BROWSER: Requests permission via navigator.geolocation.
2. OS: Retrieves GPS data from hardware chip.
3. RAM: Data is stored in volatile memory to display map.
4. TERMINATION: Tab close triggers garbage collection (data deletion).
5. SERVER: NO LOGS CREATED.

2. External API Usage (Data Processors)

To provide utility features like reverse-geocoding (finding your street address) and weather, anonymous queries are sent to trusted third-party APIs. We strip these queries of identifying metadata.

  • Nominatim (OpenStreetMap): Receives Latitude/Longitude only. No IP address or User ID is associated with the query.
  • Open-Meteo: Receives coordinates to fetch temperature data. This connection uses TLS 1.3 encryption.
  • Leaflet Maps: Fetches visual map tiles based on zoom level.

3. Cookie Policy & AdSense

We use Google AdSense to monetize this free tool. Google may place a cookie on your browser to serve relevant ads based on your general region (City/Country) or browsing history.

Crucial Distinction: Google's ad cookies operate independently of our GPS tool. We do not pass your precise GPS coordinates to the ad network. They see your IP address (general location), while you see your GPS location (precise location).

4. Your Rights (GDPR & CCPA)

Because we do not store personal data, "Right to Delete" requests are technically moot (there is nothing to delete). However, we fully support your Right to Opt-Out of non-essential tracking via our Consent Manager located in the site footer.
