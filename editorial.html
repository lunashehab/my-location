Editorial Policy & Standards

At TellMyLocation.com, our mission is to provide life-saving location data with zero ambiguity. Because our users often rely on our tools in emergency or travel scenarios, we adhere to strict standards of technical accuracy and transparency.

1. Verification Process

Every technical guide published on this platform undergoes a rigorous review process:

  • Technical Validation: Code snippets and API references (such as W3C Geolocation standards) are tested in current versions of Chrome, Safari, and Firefox before publication.
  • Safety Review: Any content related to emergency services (SOS, 911/112 protocols) is cross-referenced with official guidelines from aviation and maritime safety authorities to ensure no misleading advice is given.

2. Updates & Revisions

Technology evolves rapidly. We commit to auditing our core content quarterly. If a browser update changes how permissions operate (e.g., iOS Privacy changes), we update our guides within 30 days. The "Last Updated" date on every article reflects the most recent technical audit.

3. Authorship & Accountability

All content is produced by our in-house engineering team under the supervision of Don Odibat (Chief Architect). We do not accept sponsored posts, guest posts from unverified sources, or "pay-for-placement" links. If you spot an error, please contact our engineering team directly at don.odibat@gmail.com for an immediate correction.
