The Science of IP Geolocation

Every device connected to the internet is assigned a unique number known as an IP Address. But how does a website turn that number into a physical location on a map?

The WHOIS Database

When an Internet Service Provider (ISP) buys a block of IP addresses, they register them with a regional authority. This registration often includes the city where that block of IPs is intended to be used.

Why It is Often Inaccurate

  • The "Headquarters" Problem: An ISP might register all their IPs to their corporate HQ in a major city, even if the user is 500 miles away.
  • Dynamic IPs: ISPs frequently shuffle IP addresses. A database might think an IP is in New York, but it was re-assigned to New Jersey yesterday.

The Solution: Hardware GPS

TellMyLocation.com bypasses the inaccurate IP method and asks your device's actual GPS chip for the truth. This improves accuracy from "City Level" to "Street Level".
