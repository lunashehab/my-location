Latitude vs. Longitude: The Global Grid

Every point on Earth can be defined by two numbers. Understanding these numbers is the key to reading any map, GPS, or navigation system.

1. Latitude (The Ladder)

Latitude lines run horizontally, like the rungs of a ladder. They measure how far North or South you are.

Equator: 0° Latitude
North Pole: +90°
South Pole: -90°

If the first number in your GPS coordinate is positive, you are in the Northern Hemisphere (USA, Europe, Asia). If negative, you are in the Southern Hemisphere (Australia, South America).

2. Longitude (The Long Lines)

Longitude lines run vertically from pole to pole. They measure how far East or West you are.

Prime Meridian (London): 0° Longitude
East: Positive numbers (+)
West (Americas): Negative numbers (-)

How to Read a Coordinate

Coordinates are always written as (Latitude, Longitude).

Example: 40.7128, -74.0060 (New York City).
This means: 40 degrees North of the Equator, and 74 degrees West of London.
