The Architecture of Privacy

In the digital age, "Privacy" is often just a legal disclaimer. At TellMyLocation.com, it is an architectural decision. This guide explains the technical difference between Server-Side and Client-Side geolocation, and why it matters for your safety.

The Standard Model (Server-Side)

Most location apps work like this: Your phone sends your GPS coordinates to a central cloud server. That server processes the data, logs it in a database, and sends a map back to you. This creates a permanent record of your movement on a computer you do not own.

Our Model (Client-Side / Zero-Knowledge)

We use a different approach. All geolocation logic on this website happens inside your browser (Client-Side).

  • Request: Your browser (Chrome/Safari) asks the Operating System for location access.
  • Processing: The OS returns the coordinates directly to the JavaScript running in your tab.
  • Display: The browser draws the map.

Crucially, at no point does this data need to be sent to our backend server to be processed. The "math" happens on your phone, not our cloud.

Technical Note: We use the HTML5 navigator.geolocation.getCurrentPosition() method. This API is sandboxed by the browser, ensuring that we cannot access your location in the background once the tab is closed.

Why "Ephemeral" Matters

Because we do not have a user database, we cannot be hacked in a way that leaks your location history. There is nothing to leak. Your session is ephemeral—it exists only as long as the tab is open. Once you close it, the data is wiped from your device's RAM (Random Access Memory).
