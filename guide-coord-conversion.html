Converting Coordinates: DD to DMS

While digital systems prefer Decimal Degrees (DD), humans often communicate in Degrees, Minutes, Seconds (DMS). This guide explains the mathematical formula to convert between them manually.

The Conversion Formula

To convert a decimal coordinate (e.g., 40.7128°) to DMS:

Step 1: The Degrees
Take the whole number part.
Result: 40°

Step 2: The Minutes
Take the decimal part (0.7128) and multiply by 60.
0.7128 * 60 = 42.768
Whole number is Minutes: 42'

Step 3: The Seconds
Take the remaining decimal (0.768) and multiply by 60.
0.768 * 60 = 46.08
Result: 46.08"

Final Result: 40° 42' 46.08" N

Why 60? (Base-60 Math)

This system dates back to ancient Babylonians, who used a base-60 counting system. Just as an hour has 60 minutes and a minute has 60 seconds, a degree of latitude covers approximately 69 miles (111 km), which is subdivided into 60 "minutes" of distance.
