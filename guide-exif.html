The Invisible Danger: Geotags in Your Photos

You snap a photo of your new home and email it to a friend. You might have just sent them your exact GPS coordinates, accurate to within 3 meters. This is due to EXIF Metadata.

What is EXIF Data?

Exchangeable Image File Format (EXIF) is a standard that stores hidden data inside JPG and HEIC image files. This data includes:

  • Camera Model (e.g., iPhone 15 Pro)
  • Date and Time
  • Latitude, Longitude, and Altitude

When is it Safe? When is it Dangerous?

Social Media (Safe): Major platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and X (Twitter) automatically strip EXIF data when you upload. If you post a photo there, the location is removed.

Direct Sharing (Dangerous): If you send a photo via Email, iMessage, or AirDrop, the file is usually sent "original quality," meaning the GPS coordinates remain embedded. A recipient can download the image, view "Properties," and see exactly where you live.

How to Protect Yourself

On Android and iOS, go to your Camera settings and toggle "Save Location" to OFF if you are concerned about privacy. For existing photos, use an "EXIF Remover" tool before emailing sensitive images.
