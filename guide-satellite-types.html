GPS vs. GLONASS vs. Galileo: What Connects You?

We often use "GPS" as a generic term for location, but GPS is actually just one specific network owned by the United States. Your phone likely connects to three or four different "constellations" simultaneously to determine where you are.

1. GPS (Global Positioning System)

  • Owner: USA Space Force
  • Satellites: ~31 operational
  • Global Coverage: 100%

2. GLONASS

  • Owner: Russia (Roscosmos)
  • Specialty: Better accuracy in high northern latitudes (Canada, Scandinavia, Russia). If you are near the Arctic Circle, your phone relies heavily on GLONASS.

3. Galileo

  • Owner: European Union
  • Specialty: Civilian precision. Unlike GPS/GLONASS which were military-first, Galileo is civilian-run and offers meter-level precision for emergency services (eCall).

How "Multi-GNSS" Improves Accuracy

Modern smartphones use a "Multi-GNSS" chip. Instead of looking for 4 GPS satellites, your phone looks for any 4 satellites from any system. By pooling them together (e.g., 2 GPS + 1 Galileo + 1 GLONASS), your Time-To-Fix is faster and your accuracy is sharper.
