Does GPS Work Without Internet? A Field Guide

A common myth is that you need a data plan (4G/5G) to use GPS. This is false. This guide explains the difference between Satellite Reception and Map Data, and how to navigate when you are completely offline.

1. The Difference: GPS Chip vs. Data Connection

Your phone has two separate radios involved in location:

  • The GPS Receiver: This is a "listen-only" chip. It hears "pings" from satellites in space. It requires zero internet and costs zero money to operate. It works in the middle of the ocean or the Sahara desert.
  • The Cellular Radio: This connects to cell towers to download the visual map tiles (the drawing of streets and buildings) and traffic data.

2. How to Navigate Offline

If you are hiking or traveling without a SIM card, your blue dot will still move accurately because the GPS chip is working. However, the background might be a grey grid because the map images cannot download.

The Fix: Use TellMyLocation.com before you leave Wi-Fi. Our Progressive Web App (PWA) technology caches the core code. If you keep the tab open, you will still see your Latitude/Longitude coordinates update in real-time, even in "Airplane Mode."

3. Battery Conservation

Searching for a weak cellular signal drains battery fast. In emergencies, turn on Airplane Mode but leave Location Services ON. This disables the battery-draining cellular radio while keeping the GPS chip active for navigation.
