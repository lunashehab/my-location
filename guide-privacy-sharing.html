Privacy-Safe Location Sharing: Static vs. Live

Sharing your location is a powerful tool for safety and convenience, but it carries privacy risks. Many apps default to "Always On" tracking, building a history of your movements. This guide explains how to share your position ephemerally (temporarily) without creating a permanent log.

Method 1: The "Static Snapshot" (Safest)

This is the method used by TellMyLocation.com. When you copy your coordinates or send an SOS link, you are sharing a single point in time.

  • Pros: The recipient knows where you were at that exact second. They cannot see where you go 5 minutes later.
  • Cons: If you are moving (e.g., in an ambulance), the data becomes stale immediately.
  • Use Case: Meeting a friend at a park, sending a pickup spot, marking a hiking trailhead.

Method 2: "Live" Sharing (Higher Risk)

Apps like WhatsApp, Google Maps, and Find My (iOS) offer "Live Location." This creates an open data tunnel where your device constantly uploads your coordinates to a server.

  • Pros: Real-time tracking is essential for coordinating movement.
  • Cons: Requires trust in the server infrastructure. If you forget to turn it off, you may be tracked indefinitely.
  • Mitigation: Always set a "Time Limit" (e.g., "Share for 1 Hour") rather than "Until I turn it off."

Metadata Leakage in Links

Be wary of sharing "Shortlinks" generated by some map apps. These links often contain tracking parameters (UTM codes) that tell the recipient details about your device, battery level, or carrier. TellMyLocation generates "Clean Links" (e.g., `?lat=x&long=y`) that contain only the geography, stripping all device metadata.
